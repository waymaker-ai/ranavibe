## üí° Partnership Best Practices

### When Reaching Out:
1. **Research first:** Understand their goals
2. **Lead with value:** What's in it for them?
3. **Be specific:** Propose concrete collaboration
4. **Be authentic:** Personal touch matters
5. **Follow up:** Persistence (politely)

### When Negotiating:
1. **Win-win:** Both sides should benefit
2. **Start small:** Pilot before big commitment
3. **Document:** Clear agreements
4. **Set timelines:** Deadlines drive action
5. **Be flexible:** Adapt to their process

### When Collaborating:
1. **Deliver:** Do what you promise
2. **Communicate:** Regular updates
3. **Be responsive:** Quick turnaround
4. **Show appreciation:** Thank them publicly
5. **Track results:** Share metrics

### When Measuring:
1. **Set goals:** Define success upfront
2. **Track metrics:** Impressions, traffic, conversions
3. **Share results:** With partner
4. **Iterate:** Learn and improve
5. **Maintain:** Keep relationship warm

## üö´ Red Flags - When to Walk Away

### Don't partner if:
- They want exclusivity (locks out other providers)
- They want to change RANA's core mission
- They want control over messaging
- They don't respect open source
- They want revenue share on RANA (it's MIT licensed)
- Timeline is unrealistic
- Legal terms are onerous

### It's okay to:
- Partner on services (Waymaker can be exclusive)
- Give them preferred positioning (if they earn it)
- Co-create content (as long as it's honest)
- Share revenue from joint offerings
- Have NDA for confidential discussions
